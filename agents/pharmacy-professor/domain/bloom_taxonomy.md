# Bloom's Taxonomy for Pharmaceutical Education

Framework for classifying learning objectives and assessment items.

---

## Cognitive Domain Levels

### Level 1: Remember (Knowledge)
**Definition**: Recall facts, terms, basic concepts

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Define bioavailability
- List the four pharmacokinetic processes
- Name the drug class for metoprolol
- State the indication for warfarin

**Question Stems**:
- What is...?
- Define...
- List...
- Name...
- State...
- Identify...

**Target Percentage**: 20-30% of assessment

---

### Level 2: Understand (Comprehension)
**Definition**: Explain ideas, interpret meaning, compare concepts

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Explain how ACE inhibitors lower blood pressure
- Describe the first-pass effect
- Compare beta-1 and beta-2 selectivity
- Summarize warfarin's mechanism of action

**Question Stems**:
- Explain why...
- Describe how...
- Compare...
- Contrast...
- Summarize...
- Distinguish between...

**Target Percentage**: 25-35% of assessment

---

### Level 3: Apply (Application)
**Definition**: Use information in new situations, solve problems

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Calculate a creatinine clearance
- Determine appropriate vancomycin dosing
- Select the best antibiotic for a patient scenario
- Apply drug interaction knowledge to a case

**Question Stems**:
- Calculate...
- Determine...
- Select...
- Apply...
- Demonstrate...
- Use... to solve...

**Target Percentage**: 25-35% of assessment

---

### Level 4: Analyze (Analysis)
**Definition**: Draw connections, distinguish components, examine relationships

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Analyze why a patient failed therapy
- Differentiate between drug-induced and disease-related symptoms
- Examine the relationship between drug levels and toxicity
- Compare treatment options for a complex patient

**Question Stems**:
- Analyze why...
- Differentiate...
- Examine...
- What is the relationship between...
- Which factor most likely explains...

**Target Percentage**: 10-20% of assessment

---

### Level 5: Evaluate (Evaluation)
**Definition**: Justify decisions, critique approaches, assess outcomes

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Evaluate the appropriateness of a medication regimen
- Justify selection of one treatment over another
- Assess the significance of a drug interaction
- Critique a therapeutic plan

**Question Stems**:
- Evaluate...
- Justify...
- Assess...
- Critique...
- Which is most appropriate...
- What is the best recommendation...

**Target Percentage**: 5-15% of assessment

---

### Level 6: Create (Synthesis)
**Definition**: Design solutions, formulate plans, develop new approaches

**Pharmaceutical Examples**:
- Design a monitoring plan for a new medication
- Develop a patient education strategy
- Formulate a therapeutic plan for complex patient
- Create a drug interaction alert system

**Question Stems**:
- Design...
- Develop...
- Formulate...
- Create...
- Propose...
- How would you approach...

**Target Percentage**: 0-10% of assessment

---

## Assessment Item Examples by Level

### Remember
> Which drug class does lisinopril belong to?
> A. Beta-blocker
> B. ACE inhibitor ✓
> C. Calcium channel blocker
> D. ARB

### Understand
> Which of the following best explains why ACE inhibitors cause a dry cough?
> A. Decreased angiotensin II levels
> B. Accumulation of bradykinin ✓
> C. Increased renin secretion
> D. Reduced aldosterone production

### Apply
> A patient with CrCl 25 mL/min requires vancomycin. Initial dose is 15 mg/kg. If the patient weighs 80 kg, what is the appropriate loading dose?
> Answer: 1200 mg

### Analyze
> A patient on warfarin develops an INR of 8.5 after starting a new medication. Which newly added drug most likely caused this interaction?
> A. Acetaminophen
> B. Metronidazole ✓
> C. Omeprazole
> D. Metoprolol

### Evaluate
> Which antibiotic regimen is most appropriate for a patient with community-acquired pneumonia who has a penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis)?
> (Requires evaluating guidelines, patient factors, and alternatives)
